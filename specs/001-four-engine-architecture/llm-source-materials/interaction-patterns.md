# Interaction Pattern Library

**Purpose**: Internal reference document for developers creating LLM prompts. Provides comprehensive context about common interaction patterns in financial advice without exposing sensitive knowledge to external LLMs.

**Last Updated**: 2025-11-21
**Version**: 1.0

---

## Common User Question Types

### Factual Queries
- "What is my current net wealth?"
- "How much tax did I pay last year?"
- "What is my superannuation balance?"
- **Response Pattern**: Direct factual answer, cite calculation source, offer to explain

### Projection Queries
- "Am I on track for retirement?"
- "What will my super be worth in 20 years?"
- "Can I afford to buy a house?"
- **Response Pattern**: Present projection with assumptions, explain methodology, discuss scenarios

### Strategy Exploration
- "How could I pay less tax?"
- "What's the best way to save for retirement?"
- "Should I pay down debt or invest?"
- **Response Pattern**: Present options, explain trade-offs, discuss suitability, provide disclaimers

### Educational Queries
- "What is a franking credit?"
- "How does negative gearing work?"
- "What is the difference between super and pension?"
- **Response Pattern**: Clear explanation, use examples, link to relevant concepts, avoid personal recommendations

### Comparison Queries
- "Should I choose Strategy A or Strategy B?"
- "What's the difference between these options?"
- **Response Pattern**: Side-by-side comparison, highlight key differences, discuss suitability factors

## Educational Content Delivery Patterns

### Concept Explanation
1. Define the concept clearly
2. Provide context (why it matters)
3. Use simple examples
4. Link to related concepts
5. Avoid jargon where possible
6. Use analogies for complex topics

### Calculation Explanation
1. State the result clearly
2. Explain the methodology
3. Show key inputs and assumptions
4. Discuss limitations and caveats
5. Offer to explore scenarios

### Strategy Explanation
1. Describe the strategy
2. Explain how it works
3. Discuss benefits and risks
4. Identify when it's suitable
5. Provide disclaimers
6. Link to regulatory considerations

## Risk Communication Frameworks

### Risk Disclosure Pattern
1. Identify the risk clearly
2. Explain potential impact
3. Discuss likelihood (if quantifiable)
4. Suggest mitigation strategies
5. Ensure client understanding

### Suitability Warnings
- "This strategy may not be suitable if..."
- "Consider your personal circumstances..."
- "Seek professional advice if..."
- "This is general advice only..."

### Regulatory Disclaimers
- General advice warnings
- Personal advice scope limitations
- Product recommendation disclosures
- Fee and cost disclosures

## Goal-Setting and Strategy Discussion Flows

### Goal Identification
1. Open-ended questions about aspirations
2. Time horizon discussions
3. Priority ranking exercises
4. Quantification of goals
5. Reality checking against resources

### Strategy Development Flow
1. Present current situation analysis
2. Identify gaps and opportunities
3. Propose strategic options
4. Model outcomes
5. Discuss trade-offs
6. Recommend preferred approach
7. Address concerns and questions

### Implementation Planning
1. Break strategy into steps
2. Sequence actions logically
3. Identify dependencies
4. Set timelines
5. Assign responsibilities
6. Establish monitoring

## Conversation Management Patterns

### Active Listening
- Paraphrase to confirm understanding
- Ask clarifying questions
- Acknowledge concerns
- Validate emotions

### Information Gathering
- Use open-ended questions
- Follow up on incomplete information
- Verify understanding
- Check for missing context

### Explanation Techniques
- Start with the big picture
- Break complex topics into steps
- Use visual aids when helpful
- Check for understanding
- Encourage questions

### Handling Objections
- Acknowledge the concern
- Provide additional information
- Address underlying fears
- Offer alternatives
- Respect client autonomy

### Closing Conversations
- Summarise key points
- Confirm next steps
- Set expectations
- Provide resources
- Schedule follow-up if needed

## Tone and Language Guidelines

### Professional but Approachable
- Use clear, simple language
- Avoid unnecessary jargon
- Be respectful and empathetic
- Maintain professional boundaries

### Client-Centred
- Focus on client's goals
- Use "you" language appropriately
- Acknowledge client expertise in their own circumstances
- Empower client decision-making

### Accurate and Precise
- Use correct terminology
- Provide accurate information
- Qualify statements appropriately
- Cite sources when relevant
