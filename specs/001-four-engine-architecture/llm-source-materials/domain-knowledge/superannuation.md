# Superannuation: Contribution Strategies and Rules

**Purpose**: Internal reference document for developers creating LLM prompts. Provides comprehensive context about superannuation rules without exposing sensitive knowledge to external LLMs.

**Last Updated**: 2025-11-21
**Version**: 1.0

---

## Contribution Types

### Concessional Contributions (Pre-Tax)
- Employer Superannuation Guarantee (SG): 11% of ordinary time earnings
- Salary sacrifice contributions
- Personal deductible contributions
- Annual cap: $27,500 (2024-25)
- Tax: 15% (30% for high earners >$250k)

### Non-Concessional Contributions (Post-Tax)
- Personal after-tax contributions
- Annual cap: $110,000
- Bring-forward rule: Up to $330,000 over 3 years (if under age 67)
- Tax: 0%
- Total super balance test applies

## Contribution Strategies

### Salary Sacrifice
- Reduce taxable income
- Pay 15% tax instead of marginal rate
- Effective for incomes >$45,000
- Consider impact on SG contributions

### Personal Deductible Contributions
- Make after-tax contribution
- Claim tax deduction
- Useful for self-employed or those with unused cap

### Co-Contribution
- Government co-contribution for low-income earners
- $0.50 per $1.00 contributed (up to $500)
- Income thresholds apply

### Spouse Contributions
- Contribute to spouse's super
- Tax offset available ($540 maximum)
- Income thresholds apply

## Superannuation Guarantee (SG)

### Current Rate
- 11% of ordinary time earnings (2024-25)
- Increasing to 12% by 2025

### Eligibility
- Employees aged 18+ earning >$450/month
- Employees under 18 working >30 hours/week

### Contribution Limits
- No cap on SG contributions
- Counts toward concessional cap

## Access Rules

### Preservation Age
- Age-based access restrictions
- Ranges from 55 to 60 depending on birth date

### Conditions of Release
- Attainment of preservation age and retirement
- Attainment of age 65
- Terminal medical condition
- Permanent incapacity
- Severe financial hardship
- Compassionate grounds
- First home super saver scheme

## Superannuation Pensions

### Account-Based Pension
- Minimum drawdowns required
- Tax-free for over 60s
- Investment earnings tax-free
- Transfer balance cap: $1.9 million

### Transition to Retirement (TTR)
- Access while still working
- Minimum 4%, maximum 10% drawdown
- Tax on earnings: 15%
- Tax on withdrawals: 0% if over 60

## Superannuation Strategies

### Maximising Contributions
- Use concessional cap each year
- Consider bring-forward for non-concessional
- Monitor total super balance

### Tax Efficiency
- Accumulate in accumulation phase (15% tax)
- Convert to pension phase (0% tax) when eligible
- Consider contribution timing

### Estate Planning
- Binding death benefit nominations
- Reversionary pensions
- Tax on death benefits
- Dependents vs non-dependents
