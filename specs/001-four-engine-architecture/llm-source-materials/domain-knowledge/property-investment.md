# Property Investment: Considerations and Strategies

**Purpose**: Internal reference document for developers creating LLM prompts. Provides comprehensive context about property investment without exposing sensitive knowledge to external LLMs.

**Last Updated**: 2025-11-21
**Version**: 1.0

---

## Property Investment Types

### Residential Investment Property
- Houses and units
- Rental income assessable
- Expenses deductible
- CGT on sale
- Land tax considerations

### Commercial Property
- Office, retail, industrial
- Longer leases typically
- Higher yields potentially
- Less liquid than residential

### Property Trusts (REITs)
- Listed property trusts
- Unlisted property trusts
- Diversification benefits
- Professional management

## Investment Considerations

### Cash Flow Analysis
- Rental income
- Operating expenses (rates, insurance, maintenance, management)
- Loan interest
- Net rental yield
- Negative vs positive gearing

### Capital Growth Potential
- Location factors
- Property type and condition
- Market cycles
- Development potential
- Infrastructure projects

### Tax Benefits
- Negative gearing (interest deductibility)
- Depreciation claims
- CGT discount (50% if held >12 months)
- Main residence exemption (if applicable)

### Risks
- Market volatility
- Vacancy risk
- Interest rate risk
- Property-specific risks
- Liquidity constraints

## Financing Strategies

### Loan Structures
- Principal and interest vs interest-only
- Fixed vs variable rates
- Offset accounts
- Redraw facilities

### Loan-to-Value Ratio (LVR)
- Typical maximum: 80% (avoid LMI)
- Higher LVR possible with LMI
- Investment property rates typically higher

### Debt Serviceability
- Rental income assessment (typically 80% of rent)
- Interest rate buffers
- Other income and expenses
- APRA serviceability requirements

## Property Investment Strategies

### Buy and Hold
- Long-term capital growth focus
- Rental income over time
- Tax benefits
- Requires long-term commitment

### Renovation and Development
- Add value through improvements
- Higher risk and complexity
- Potential for higher returns
- Requires expertise and capital

### Property Flipping
- Short-term buy and sell
- CGT applies (no discount if <12 months)
- Higher transaction costs
- Requires market timing skills

## Tax Considerations

### Negative Gearing
- Interest and expenses deductible
- Losses offset against other income
- CGT on sale (with discount)

### Depreciation
- Building depreciation (2.5% per year)
- Plant and equipment depreciation
- Quantity surveyor reports
- Diminishing value vs prime cost

### CGT Planning
- Main residence exemption (if applicable)
- 50% discount if held >12 months
- Timing of sale
- Estate planning considerations

## Due Diligence

### Property Research
- Location analysis
- Comparable sales
- Rental yields
- Growth history
- Future development plans

### Financial Analysis
- Purchase price
- Renovation costs (if any)
- Holding costs
- Expected rental income
- Exit strategy

### Legal Considerations
- Title search
- Building inspections
- Strata reports (if applicable)
- Zoning and restrictions
