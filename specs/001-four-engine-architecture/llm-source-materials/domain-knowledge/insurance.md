## Life Insurance Types

### Term Life Insurance
- Death benefit only
- Fixed term (e.g., 20 years)
- Level or stepped premiums
- No cash value
- Most cost-effective option

### Total and Permanent Disability (TPD)
- Lump sum on permanent disability
- Own occupation vs any occupation definitions
- Can be standalone or linked to life insurance
- Important for income protection

### Trauma Insurance
- Lump sum on specified medical conditions
- Covers cancer, heart attack, stroke, etc.
- No need to be unable to work
- Can be used for medical expenses or lifestyle

### Income Protection
- Monthly benefit while unable to work
- Typically 75% of pre-disability income
- Waiting periods (30, 60, 90 days)
- Benefit periods (2 years, 5 years, to age 65)
- Own occupation vs any occupation

## Insurance Structures

### Retail Insurance
- Individual policies
- Through financial advisers or direct
- Underwritten at application
- Can be tailored to needs

### Group Insurance
- Through superannuation funds
- Automatic acceptance (within limits)
- Lower premiums potentially
- Less flexible

### Hybrid Structures
- Combination of retail and group
- Optimise cost and coverage
- Consider tax implications

## Suitability Considerations

### Life Insurance Needs
- Dependents and their needs
- Outstanding debts
- Education costs
- Final expenses
- Estate planning

### Income Protection Needs
- Current income level
- Expenses and commitments
- Other income sources
- Waiting period capacity
- Benefit period requirements

### TPD and Trauma Needs
- Medical expenses
- Lifestyle adjustments
- Debt repayment
- Income replacement (if no income protection)

## Tax Considerations

### Premiums
- Life insurance: Generally not deductible (personal)
- Income protection: Deductible
- TPD: Generally not deductible (personal)
- Trauma: Generally not deductible (personal)
- Through super: Concessional tax treatment

### Benefits
- Life insurance: Generally tax-free to beneficiaries
- Income protection: Taxable as income
- TPD: Tax-free if through super, potentially taxable if personal
- Trauma: Generally tax-free

### Superannuation
- Insurance through super
- Premiums from super balance
- Tax considerations on benefits
- Access restrictions

## Insurance Strategies

### Needs Analysis
- Calculate required coverage
- Consider existing coverage
- Identify gaps
- Prioritise needs

### Cost Optimisation
- Compare retail vs group
- Consider hybrid structures
- Review regularly
- Adjust as circumstances change

### Risk Management
- Adequate coverage levels
- Appropriate waiting periods
- Suitable benefit periods
- Regular reviews

## Common Insurance Scenarios

### Young Families
- High life insurance needs
- Income protection important
- Consider trauma insurance
- Review as children grow

### Pre-Retirement
- Reducing life insurance needs
- Income protection less critical
- Focus on health insurance
- Estate planning considerations

### Business Owners
- Key person insurance
- Buy-sell agreements
- Business loan protection
- Income protection critical
