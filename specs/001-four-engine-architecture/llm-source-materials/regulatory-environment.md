## ASIC Regulatory Framework

### Regulatory Guide 175 (RG 175): Financial Advice
- Sets out requirements for providing financial product advice
- Defines personal advice vs general advice
- Establishes Best Interest Duty requirements
- Outlines disclosure obligations
- Defines appropriate advice requirements

### Personal Advice vs General Advice

**Personal Advice**
- Takes into account one or more of the client's objectives, financial situation, or needs
- Requires Statement of Advice (SoA)
- Subject to Best Interest Duty
- Requires AFSL

**General Advice**
- Does not take into account client's personal circumstances
- Warning required: "This advice is general and does not take into account your objectives, financial situation or needs"
- No SoA required
- Still requires AFSL

## Best Interest Duty (BID)

### Section 961B Requirements
1. Act in the best interests of the client
2. Provide appropriate advice
3. Warn client if advice is limited in scope
4. Prioritise client interests over own interests

### Safe Harbour Steps (s961B(2))
1. Identify client's objectives, financial situation, and needs
2. Identify subject matter of advice
3. Make reasonable inquiries to obtain complete information
4. Assess whether adviser has required knowledge and skills
5. Conduct reasonable investigation
6. Base all judgements on client's relevant circumstances
7. Take steps to satisfy client understanding and consent
8. Only recommend products if satisfied they are in client's best interests

## Licensing Requirements

### Australian Financial Services Licence (AFSL)
- Required for providing financial product advice
- Required for dealing in financial products
- Required for making a market
- Required for operating a registered scheme
- Issued by ASIC
- Ongoing compliance obligations

### Australian Credit Licence (ACL)
- Required for credit activities
- Credit assistance (broking)
- Credit provision (lending)
- Issued by ASIC
- Separate from AFSL

## Consumer Protection Laws

### Corporations Act 2001
- Financial services provisions
- Best Interest Duty
- Disclosure requirements
- Product intervention powers

### National Consumer Credit Protection Act 2009
- Responsible lending obligations
- Credit assessment requirements
- Hardship provisions

### Australian Consumer Law (ACL)
- Unfair contract terms
- Misleading and deceptive conduct
- Unconscionable conduct
- Consumer guarantees

## Privacy and Data Handling

### Privacy Act 1988
- Australian Privacy Principles (APPs)
- Collection, use, and disclosure of personal information
- Data security requirements
- Access and correction rights
- Privacy policy requirements

### Notifiable Data Breaches Scheme
- Mandatory notification of eligible data breaches
- Notification to OAIC and affected individuals
- Assessment and remediation requirements

## Regulatory Bodies

### Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC)
- Market conduct regulator
- Financial services licensing
- Consumer protection
- Enforcement actions

### Australian Prudential Regulation Authority (APRA)
- Prudential regulation
- Banks, insurers, superannuation funds
- Financial stability

### Australian Taxation Office (ATO)
- Tax administration
- Superannuation compliance
- Tax rulings and guidance

### Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC)
- Privacy regulation
- Freedom of information
- Data breach notifications

## Compliance Obligations

### Ongoing Compliance
- Maintain AFSL/ACL conditions
- Continuing professional development (CPD)
- Compliance monitoring and reporting
- Record keeping
- Client file maintenance

### Breach Reporting
- Reportable situations to ASIC
- Significant breaches
- Within 30 days of becoming aware
- Remediation requirements
