## Stages of Financial Advice

### 1. Fact-Finding (Discovery)
- Gather client personal information (age, dependents, employment)
- Understand financial position (assets, liabilities, income, expenses)
- Identify goals and objectives (short-term, medium-term, long-term)
- Assess risk tolerance and capacity
- Understand time horizons for different goals
- Document current strategies and products

### 2. Needs Analysis
- Identify gaps between current position and goals
- Prioritise goals based on importance and urgency
- Assess capacity to achieve goals with current strategies
- Identify risks and opportunities
- Consider life stage factors (accumulation, preservation, retirement)

### 3. Strategy Development
- Develop strategic recommendations aligned with goals
- Consider multiple strategy options
- Model financial outcomes using projections
- Assess tax implications
- Evaluate risk/return trade-offs
- Consider implementation complexity

### 4. Implementation
- Create action plan with priorities
- Sequence implementation steps
- Identify required documentation and forms
- Coordinate with product providers
- Set up monitoring and review schedules

### 5. Review and Monitoring
- Regular reviews (typically annual)
- Update financial position
- Assess progress toward goals
- Adjust strategies as circumstances change
- Update projections and models

## Adviser Responsibilities and Obligations

### Best Interest Duty (BID)
- Act in the best interests of the client (s961B Corporations Act)
- Prioritise client interests over own interests
- Provide appropriate advice
- Warn client if advice is limited in scope

### Know Your Client (KYC)
- Understand client's personal circumstances
- Understand client's objectives
- Understand client's financial situation
- Understand client's needs

### Appropriate Advice
- Advice must be appropriate to the client
- Must be based on client's relevant circumstances
- Must consider client's objectives, financial situation, and needs

### Disclosure Requirements
- Financial Services Guide (FSG)
- Statement of Advice (SoA) for personal advice
- Product Disclosure Statement (PDS) when recommending products
- Fee disclosure statements
- Ongoing fee arrangements

## Client Communication Patterns

### Initial Consultation
- Establish rapport and trust
- Explain advice process and costs
- Gather comprehensive information
- Set expectations about advice scope

### Strategy Presentation
- Explain recommendations clearly
- Use visual aids (charts, graphs, projections)
- Address questions and concerns
- Explain risks and limitations
- Obtain client understanding and agreement

### Ongoing Communication
- Regular check-ins and updates
- Respond promptly to queries
- Explain changes in circumstances
- Provide educational content
- Maintain professional boundaries

## Documentation Requirements

### Statement of Advice (SoA)
- Client's relevant circumstances
- Basis for advice
- Recommendations and reasons
- Risks and warnings
- Costs and benefits
- Conflicts of interest
- Complaints handling

### Record Keeping
- Client files and notes
- Fact-finding documents
- Calculations and projections
- Product research and comparisons
- Client communications
- Review documentation
