## Overview

The Australian financial system is regulated by multiple bodies including the Australian Prudential Regulation Authority (APRA), the Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC), and the Australian Taxation Office (ATO).

## Key Financial Institutions

### Banks
- Major banks: Commonwealth Bank, ANZ, Westpac, NAB
- Regional banks and credit unions
- Authorised Deposit-Taking Institutions (ADIs) regulated by APRA

### Superannuation Funds
- Retail funds (run by financial institutions)
- Industry funds (run by employer/union groups)
- Self-Managed Superannuation Funds (SMSFs)
- Public sector funds

### Investment Vehicles
- Managed funds (unit trusts)
- Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs)
- Listed Investment Companies (LICs)
- Direct shares (ASX-listed)
- Property investment trusts (REITs)

## Key Financial Concepts

### Tax System Fundamentals

**PAYG (Pay As You Go)**
- Withholding tax on employment income
- Instalment system for business income
- Tax brackets: 0%, 19%, 32.5%, 37%, 45% (plus Medicare levy 2%)

**Capital Gains Tax (CGT)**
- Applies to disposal of assets acquired after 19 September 1985
- 50% discount for assets held >12 months (individuals)
- Main residence exemption
- CGT events include sale, gift, loss/destruction

**Superannuation**
- Compulsory employer contributions (Superannuation Guarantee)
- Concessional contributions (pre-tax): $27,500 cap
- Non-concessional contributions (post-tax): $110,000 cap
- Preservation age: 55-60 depending on birth date
- Tax on contributions: 15% (concessional), 0% (non-concessional)
- Tax on earnings: 15% (accumulation), 0% (pension phase)

### Investment Structures

**Individual Ownership**
- Direct ownership of assets
- Taxed at marginal rates
- Full CGT discount eligibility

**Trusts**
- Discretionary trusts (family trusts)
- Unit trusts
- Tax flow-through structure
- Beneficiaries taxed on distributions

**Companies**
- Corporate tax rate: 30% (25% for small businesses <$50M turnover)
- Dividend imputation (franking credits)
- Limited liability protection

**Superannuation**
- 15% tax on contributions and earnings
- 0% tax in pension phase
- Restrictions on access until preservation age

## Financial Products

- Transaction accounts
- Savings accounts

- Cash management accounts

### Investment Products
- Shares (direct and via managed funds)

- Investment property loans

- Credit cards

## Regulatory Framework

### Key Regulators
- **APRA**: Prudential regulation of banks, insurers, superannuation
- **ASIC**: Market conduct, financial services licensing, consumer protection
- **ATO**: Tax administration and superannuation compliance

### Licensing Requirements
- **AFSL** (Australian Financial Services Licence): Required for providing financial product advice
- **ACL** (Australian Credit Licence): Required for credit activities
- **RG 175**: ASIC Regulatory Guide for financial advice
